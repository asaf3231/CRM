## 4. Decisions made

- **`Sheet` as a Radix Dialog** (not a custom portal div): Radix gives focus-trap, Esc-close, and overlay-click-close for free — accessible out of the box, consistent with the other primitives in the codebase.
- **`enabled: open` on the detail query**: prevents a stale fetch when no lead is selected; the detail is only fetched when the drawer is actually open (lead !== null).
- **`staleTime: 5min` on getLeadDetail**: avoids a re-fetch flicker if the user closes and re-opens the same lead drawer within 5 minutes.
- **`toStoreLead` synthetic ids start at 9000**: collision-proof against the `ld-001`–`ld-020` range in `MOCK_LEADS`. A counter (`_discoverySeq`) ensures each discovered lead gets a unique id even across multiple runs.
- **Dedup by domain in `allLeads` (not by id)**: the same company can appear in both `MOCK_LEADS` (id `ld-001`) and `discoveredLeads` (id `disc-9001`) after a discovery run; domain is the stable unique key across both sources, matching how `findMoreLeads` deduplicates.
- **Contacts stopPropagation on the domain link only**: clicking the company name cell (outside the link) correctly opens the drawer; only the `<a>` tag stops propagation so it opens in a new tab without also opening the drawer.

## 5. Deviations from the brief

None. All three deliverables (drawer, cross-screen state, polish) were implemented as specified.

One minor style call: the `SheetDescription` in the drawer header uses `asChild` with a `<div>` (instead of a `<p>`) to avoid a DOM nesting warning from Radix (`<p>` cannot contain `<a>` when Radix renders `<p>` as the default). This is a correct HTML fix, not a regression.
